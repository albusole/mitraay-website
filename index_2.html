Food that supports the work

The Treehouse — for the facilitator

The 4-person treehouse is reserved separately from participant dorms. It's private prep space, decompression room, and a signal to your group that the person guiding them is held differently. Facilitators who are well-rested run better sessions. We know this.

Plan a School Trip
Packages

Day trip or overnight — both work.

Option 1

Day Trip

Arrive morning, leave before sunset. Structured farm activities + thali lunch. No overnight logistics.

  • Farm walk & nursery tour
  • Composting, planting, kitchen garden
  • Farm thali lunch (per head)
  • Up to 100 students (batched)
Option 2

Overnight Stay

2 days, 1 night. Students experience sunrise, farm morning routines, and real independence.

  • Day 1: arrival, farm learning, bonfire
  • Night: dormitory stay (22–28)
  • Day 2: sunrise walk, Raigad add-on
  • Teacher accommodation: FREE
Learning outcomes

Outcomes. Not just activities.

01

Where food comes from

From seed to plate — students plant, grow, harvest, and eat. Not a simulation.

02

Environmental awareness

Composting, water conservation, biodiversity walks. Sahyadri ecology made tangible.

03

Maratha history

Raigad Fort add-on brings textbook history to life with a guide who knows the stories.

04

Team and community

Shared meals, dorms, routines. The farm creates group cohesion classrooms rarely do.

05

Self-reliance

Overnight students manage their own packing, routine, and space.

06

Respect for rural life

Meeting the farmers — something most urban students have never experienced up close.

Teacher accommodation is free.

Accompanying teachers stay free of charge on overnight school trips. Food for teachers billed at the same per-head rate as students.
